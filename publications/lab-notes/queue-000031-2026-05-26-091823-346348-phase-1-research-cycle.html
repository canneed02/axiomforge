### **1. Falsifiable Claim**
*"A self-supervised system that iteratively refines hypotheses using a bounded memory of past experiments (≤100 entries) can reduce the rate of redundant or contradictory research actions by ≥30% compared to a naive episodic memory baseline, as measured by the *Research Redundancy Score* (RRS) over 100 simulated research cycles."*

**Key Terms:**
- *Research Redundancy Score (RRS)*: A metric quantifying the fraction of actions that either (a) repeat prior failed experiments without modification, or (b) contradict prior confirmed hypotheses (e.g., proposing a falsified claim as novel).
- *Self-supervised refinement*: The system autonomously updates its hypothesis space based on outcomes (success/failure) of prior experiments, without external labels.

---

### **2. Experiment/Proof Harness**
**Setup:**
- **Environment**: Simulated research domain (e.g., synthetic biology, materials science) with:
  - A finite hypothesis space (e.g., 100 possible claims).
  - Stochastic experiment outcomes (e.g., 20% false positives/negatives).
  - A "ground truth" subset of 10 valid hypotheses (unknown to the system).
- **Baselines**:
  1. *Naive Episodic Memory*: Stores all prior experiments but does not refine hypotheses.
  2. *Random Action*: Selects experiments uniformly at random.
- **Proposed System**:
  - *Memory*: Bounded FIFO queue (100 entries) of experiment outcomes (claim, action, result).
  - *Refinement*: After each experiment, updates a Bayesian belief network over hypotheses, pruning those with ≥3 contradictory outcomes and reinforcing those with ≥3 supporting outcomes.
  - *Action Selection*: Uses Thompson sampling to balance exploration (novel hypotheses) and exploitation (high-confidence hypotheses).

**Evaluation Metrics:**
- *RRS* (primary): Defined above.
- *Discovery Rate*: Fraction of ground-truth hypotheses identified within 100 cycles.
- *Memory Efficiency*: Number of unique hypotheses explored per memory entry.

**Hypothesis:**
The proposed system will achieve:
- RRS ≤ 0.2 (vs. ≥0.5 for baselines).
- Discovery rate ≥ 0.6 (vs. ≤0.3 for baselines).

### **3. Failure Modes**
